Chicken Croquettes



This recipe makes flavorful turkey or chicken croquettes that are crisp on the outside but moist on the inside.
The way to keep the croquettes moist is by adding a homemade white sauce to the chicken mixture.


Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 3 cups finely chopped cooked chicken
  • 1 & 1/2 cups seasoned bread crumbs, divided
  • 2 beaten eggs
  • 1/4 cup minced onion
  • 1 tablespoon dried parsley
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/8 teaspoon celery seed
  • 1/8 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup oil, or as needed

Steps

  1. Melt butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Whisk flour into melted butter; cook and stir until flour is
    cooked and a paste forms, about 1 minute. Slowly add milk and chicken broth into butter mixture, whisking constantly, until
    mixture is thick and smooth, 5 to 10 minutes. Remove sauce from heat and cool slightly, about 10 minutes.
  2. Stir chicken, 1 cup bread crumbs, eggs, onion, parsley, garlic powder, celery seed, salt, and black pepper together in a bowl;
    add cooled sauce to chicken mixture and stir until well combined. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate until
    chilled and firm, at least 2 hours.
  3. Shape chilled chicken mixture into 6 patties. Pour remaining bread crumbs into a shallow bowl and roll each chicken patty in bread
    crumbs to coat completely.
  4. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook patties in hot oil, turning once, until patties are cooked through and
    golden brown on each side, about 10 minutes. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read at least
    165 degrees F (74 degrees C).
